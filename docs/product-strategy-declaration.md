# Product Strategy Declaration

## §0 Authority Limit

This document is non-executive and does not enter the Source of Truth hierarchy. If it conflicts with live queue, closeout-pack plan, product contracts, validators, or active goal wording, those sources win. It does not authorize implementation, ledger changes, contract changes, validator changes, or production_ready claims.

## §1 North Star

Law Firm OS is a matter-first legal work OS: every work object belongs to a Matter or an approved People/HR evidence context; fail-closed permission and hash-chain audit precede access; AI output is never final without human review; the core remains multi-tenant-capable while AMIC is treated as the first tenant.

## §2 Users

Attorneys, partners, paralegals, HR users, finance users, client users, candidate users, external experts, admins, and evidence reviewers are first-class audiences.

## §5 Non-Goals

No HRX split product, no production_ready weakening, no Claude final-approval authority, no closed-pack reinterpretation, no TUW governance replacement, no real client data in validation.

## §6 Success Criteria

Deployment-invariant criteria: matter traceability, tenant isolation, permission/audit first behavior, human-reviewed AI, source-indexed evidence, and validator-backed closeout.

Decision-dependent criteria: MAT-DEC-01 is recorded as first-tenant alignment, so internal deployment requirements are absorbed without removing SaaS-capable boundaries.

## §7 Decision Records

MAT-DEC-01 and MAT-DEC-02 are decided. MAT-DEC-03 is deferred. MAT-DEC-04 is decided: the product brand is matter, the UI brand is matter by AMIC, Law Firm OS remains the platform/repository code name, and machine identifiers remain unchanged. MAT-DEC-06 and MAT-DEC-07 authorize HRX registration and runtime gate layer respectively.

## §8 Naming

Final naming convention: matter is the product brand, matter by AMIC is the UI brand, Law Firm OS is the platform/repository code name, and machine identifiers such as `law-firm-os` are not renamed.
